Create a personalized training plan for the athlete's upcoming week based on their current fitness, fatigue, goals, and real-life schedule.

## Data to Retrieve

Pull from Intervals.icu:

- Current fitness summary (CTL, ATL, TSB)
- Activities from the past 14 days (to understand recent patterns)
- Wellness data from the past 7 days
- Any existing events/planned workouts already on the calendar for the coming week

## Schedule Awareness

Before designing the week, understand when the athlete can actually train. This is critical for creating plans they can follow.

### Check Historical Patterns

Look at the past 14 days of activities and note:
- Which days of the week the athlete typically rides
- How long those sessions are (do weekday rides cluster around 60-90 min while weekends are longer?)
- Whether there are patterns suggesting time constraints (e.g., only short rides on certain days)

### Ask About This Week's Availability

Use AskUserQuestion to confirm the coming week's schedule:

- **Which days can you ride this week?** (Some weeks differ from the usual pattern — travel, meetings, family commitments)
- **How much time do you have on each of those days?** (e.g., "Tuesday and Thursday I have 90 minutes max, Saturday is open for a long ride")
- **Any days that are definitely off?** (commitments that make training impossible)

If the athlete has already shared their weekly availability pattern (e.g., during onboarding), reference that as the default and ask only whether this week has any changes from normal.

### Fit Workouts to Available Time

When designing the week:
- Assign the most important/intense sessions to the days with the most available time
- If a day only has 60 minutes, don't plan a 90-minute sweetspot session — pick something that fits
- Place hard sessions on days where the athlete has enough time for warm-up, the main set, and cool-down
- Long endurance rides go on the day with the largest time window (usually weekends)
- Recovery rides and rest days go on time-constrained days or after hard blocks
- Account for non-cycling activities (running, strength) if known — don't stack a hard ride on a strength training day

## Planning Process

1. **Assess readiness**: Based on TSB, wellness data, and recent training load, determine whether this should be a build week, maintenance week, or recovery week.

2. **Check the calendar**: Note any events already scheduled (group rides, races, planned workouts). Build around these.

3. **Design the week**: Create a day-by-day plan for the next 7 days. For each day, specify:
   - Workout type (endurance, sweetspot, threshold, VO2max, recovery, rest)
   - Duration (fitted to that day's available time)
   - Key targets (power zone, specific intervals, RPE)
   - Brief purpose/rationale

4. **Weekly targets**: Provide overall targets for the week:
   - Total hours and TSS target
   - Expected CTL change
   - Planned intensity distribution

5. **Flexibility notes**: Identify which sessions are highest priority (don't skip these) and which can be shortened or dropped if life gets in the way. Suggest how to rearrange if a planned day falls through.

Reference the workout templates in the cycling-coaching skill for specific workout structures.

Offer to add the planned workouts to their Intervals.icu calendar using the event creation tools.
