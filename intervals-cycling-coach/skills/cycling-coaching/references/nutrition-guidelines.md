# Nutrition Guidelines for Endurance Cyclists

Quick-reference guide for the coaching skill. All values are starting points — adjust based on the individual athlete's weight, training load, and response.

## On-Bike Fueling Targets

### Carbohydrate Intake by Ride Type

| Ride Type         | Duration | Intensity | Carbs/Hour Target                 |
| ----------------- | -------- | --------- | --------------------------------- |
| Easy / Recovery   | < 1.5h   | Z1-Z2     | Water only or 20-30g              |
| Endurance         | 1.5-3h   | Z2        | 40-60g                            |
| Long endurance    | 3-5h     | Z2-Z3     | 60-80g                            |
| Hard intervals    | 1-2h     | Z4-Z5     | 40-60g                            |
| Race / Gran Fondo | 3-6h     | Mixed     | 80-100g (gut-trained: up to 120g) |

### Fueling Timing

- **3-4h pre-ride**: Large carb-rich meal (2-3g carb/kg body weight)
- **1-2h pre-ride**: Light snack (30-50g carbs), easy to digest
- **During ride**: Start fueling within first 30-45 minutes for rides over 90 minutes
- **Post-ride (0-30 min)**: 20-30g protein + 1-1.2g carb/kg body weight
- **Post-ride (1-2h)**: Full meal with protein, carbs, and vegetables

### Hydration

- **Baseline**: 500-750ml/hour in moderate conditions
- **Hot weather**: Up to 1000ml/hour, add electrolytes
- **Electrolytes**: 500-1000mg sodium per hour in hot conditions or for heavy sweaters
- **Pre-ride**: 500ml water in the 2 hours before riding

## Daily Nutrition Framework

### Macronutrient Targets (per kg body weight)

| Training Day Type        | Carbs (g/kg) | Protein (g/kg) | Fat (g/kg) |
| ------------------------ | ------------ | -------------- | ---------- |
| Rest day                 | 3-4          | 1.6-2.0        | 1.0-1.2    |
| Easy / Recovery ride     | 4-5          | 1.6-2.0        | 1.0-1.2    |
| Moderate training day    | 5-7          | 1.8-2.0        | 0.8-1.0    |
| Hard / Long training day | 7-10         | 1.8-2.2        | 0.8-1.0    |
| Double day or race       | 8-12         | 2.0-2.2        | 0.8-1.0    |

### Calorie Estimation from Training

Use ride kilojoules from Intervals.icu as the best estimate of cycling caloric expenditure (1 kJ ≈ 1 kcal for cycling due to ~25% mechanical efficiency).

### Daily Calorie Needs (approximate)

- **BMR**: Use Mifflin-St Jeor equation
- **Activity factor**: 1.4-1.6 for moderate training days, 1.6-1.9 for hard days
- **Training calories**: Add ride kJ directly

## Periodized Nutrition Strategies

### By Training Phase

- **Base phase**: Moderate carbs, emphasize nutrient density, practice fueling habits for long rides
- **Build phase**: Increase carbs on intensity days, practice race-day nutrition during hard sessions
- **Peak/Race phase**: High carb availability, nothing new on race day, carb-load 24-48h before events

### By Day Type (Fuel for the Work)

- **Hard training day**: High carb before, during, and after. Don't restrict.
- **Easy training day**: Moderate carbs, can ride fasted for short easy sessions if desired
- **Rest day**: Lower carbs (but not low-carb), focus on protein for repair, vegetables, whole foods
- **Pre-race day**: Carb-load (8-10g/kg), reduce fiber, familiar foods only

## Body Composition Notes

- Sustainable fat loss while training: max 200-300 kcal deficit on easy/rest days only
- Never restrict on hard training days — this impairs adaptation and recovery
- Target rate: 0.3-0.5 kg/week maximum
- Monitor power output — if watts drop, you're cutting too aggressively
- Off-season is the best time for more aggressive body composition work, not during build/peak phases

## Common Sports Nutrition Products

### During Ride

- **Gels**: 20-30g carbs each, easy to carry, take with water
- **Drink mix**: 40-80g carbs per bottle, combines hydration and fueling
- **Bars**: 30-50g carbs, better for steady endurance rides
- **Rice cakes / real food**: Good for long Z2 rides, less practical for intervals

### Recovery

- **Recovery shake**: 20-30g protein + 40-60g carbs within 30 minutes
- **Chocolate milk**: ~20g protein + ~40g carbs per 500ml
- **Real food meal**: Ideal within 1-2 hours post-ride

### Supplements Worth Considering

- **Caffeine**: 3-6mg/kg, 30-60 min pre-ride for hard sessions or events
- **Creatine**: 3-5g/day, may benefit high-intensity efforts and recovery
- **Vitamin D**: If training indoors frequently or in northern latitudes
- **Iron**: Only if deficient (get blood work, don't supplement blindly)
- **Beta-alanine**: 3-6g/day, may help with repeated hard efforts
